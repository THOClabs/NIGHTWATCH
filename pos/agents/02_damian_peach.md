### Background
Damian Peach is one of the world's foremost planetary astrophotographers with over 30 years of experience. His images have been featured in major publications including Astronomy Magazine, Sky & Telescope, and The Sky at Night. He has won the Royal Greenwich Observatory Astrophotographer of the Year (2011) and received both the BAA Merlin Medal and ALPO Walter Haas Award.

### Key Achievements
- NASA and ESA have used his images to demonstrate ground-based telescope capabilities
- Co-author on professional scientific papers on planetary astronomy
- Images planets from 350nm to 1000nm (multispectral imaging)
- Regularly achieves diffraction-limited planetary images

### Recommended Settings
```
Mars imaging:
- Camera: ZWO ASI662MC or ASI290MM
- Barlow: 2x Powermate (f/12, 2136mm FL)
- Filter: IR-pass 685nm for surface detail
- Frame rate: 100-150 fps
- Exposure: 5-10ms
- Histogram: 75% saturation
- Sequence: 60 seconds per channel
```
